Market Value Trends — Key Observations
  • ALL aircraft types rising in value — IBA, Cirium, Oliver Wyman consensus: no reversal expected before 2028–2030. Combined Boeing/Airbus backlog ~14,884 = ~11 years of production at current rates. OEM supply/demand normalisation unlikely before 2031–2034 (Oliver Wyman).
  • A320ceo values surging unexpectedly — IBA Jan 2026: Half-Life Base Value increased 25–118% across vintage ranges — the largest single update for any aircraft type IBA has recorded. Operators extending leases 5–8 years beyond planned disposal due to neo delivery delays.
  • 777-300ER: +78% MV appreciation since 2022 — driven by 777X certification delays (first delivery Q1 2027 at earliest). GE90-115B engine values up 60% since 2023; spot lease US$170–185K/mo. P2F demand creates additional floor.
  • 777-200ER: end-of-life asset — 422 built (production ended 2013); ~200+ active, avg age 20+ yrs. CMV US$5–8M for mid-2000s vintages (vs US$120M+ at peak). 59 scrapped (highest of any 777 variant). No P2F programme for standard -200ER. United retiring majority by Dec 2027; BA retiring 35 by 2029. Exception: American Airlines retrofitting 47 aircraft with new Flagship Suite — operating into 2030s.
  • GTF vs LEAP premium — ~700 A320neo-family aircraft grounded for GTF PW1100G inspections mid-2025. LEAP-1A-powered variants trade at premium. GTF engines represent ~50.5% of aircraft value (vs ~42.9% for LEAP). GTF ground-day rates ~12% vs upper-30s% for LEAP.
  • 787-9 is the most sought-after widebody — Market Values trending ABOVE Base Values (abnormal premium). Production at 8/mo; target 10/mo in 2026. New-gen widebody values up avg 11.6% since 2021 (IBA).
  • E-Jet E2 gaining momentum — Record 157 E2 orders in 2025 (best year in programme history). Embraer total backlog: US$31.6B (record). Avelo Airlines 50-firm US market breakthrough (Sep 2025).
  • ATR 72-600 values rising on supply constraint — ATR delivered only 32 aircraft in 2025 (vs 88 peak in 2015). IBA notes market values trending above base values — a sign of genuine market tightness. New delivery MV ~US$22.5M. ATR is the only active turboprop OEM (DHC Dash 8 production halted 2022), giving it sole-manufacturer pricing power. PW127XT engine option (2025) enhances residual value for new deliveries.

Fleet Age Implications for Consulting
  • Bangkok Airways — oldest fleet in SE Asia target set (14.2 yrs). Strong candidate for fleet renewal advisory. No current orders on book.
  • Garuda Indonesia — post-restructuring with ageing fleet (11.5 yrs). Likely to require fleet planning and financing advisory as it stabilises.
  • Thai Airways — post-restructuring with 50 aircraft on order. Fleet transition management and delivery oversight are consulting opportunities.
  • Emirates A380 transition — 262-aircraft fleet with avg age 10.2 yrs facing complex widebody fleet transition. 777X delays create short-term capacity challenges.
  • 777-200ER fleet retirements — ~200+ active globally (avg age 20+ yrs). United Airlines (55) retiring majority by Dec 2027 (PW4090 engine issues accelerating). British Airways retiring 35 by 2029. American Airlines (47) counter-trend: full cabin retrofit into 2030s. Thai Airways retains small sub-fleet. No P2F programme for -200ER creates disposal/parting-out advisory opportunities. Replacement aircraft: 787-9/10, A350-900.
  • Saudi Arabia expansion — Riyadh Air greenfield startup plus Saudia expansion under Vision 2030 creates substantial consulting demand for fleet planning, AOC establishment, and operational setup.
  • VietJet & Lion Air — massive narrowbody backlogs (286 and 180 respectively) require delivery management, financing, and fleet planning support.
  • ATR turboprop market — ATR is the only active turboprop manufacturer (63% market share; DHC Dash 8 production halted 2022). Key SE Asia operator: Wings Air (77 aircraft, largest globally). Pacific Islands airlines (Air Tahiti, PNG Air, Air New Zealand) depend on ATR. Supply-constrained market (32 deliveries in 2025 vs 88 peak in 2015) creates fleet planning advisory opportunities for regional carriers awaiting delivery slots.
  • Qantas — massive fleet renewal — ~214 aircraft on order (A321XLR replacing 737-800, A350-1000 for Project Sunrise ultra-long-haul, 787-9/10 expansion). Delivery management, cabin programme oversight, and Western Sydney Airport launch (Oct 2026) all create consulting demand. A380 replacement by A350-1000LR from ~FY2032.
  • Fiji Airways — A330 replacement decision imminent — 5× A330s (avg ~9.5 yrs) facing replacement; A330neo rejected; evaluating A350 vs 787. New CEO to finalise in 2026. Young A350-900 fleet (4 aircraft, ~3.4 yrs) performing well. Fleet advisory and financing support for widebody decision is a direct consulting opportunity.
  • Solomon Airlines — financial sustainability crisis — 7-aircraft fleet with no orders; Qantas BNE-HIR competition eroding yields; single Dash 8-100 creates domestic reliability risk; new CEO (Feb 2026). Fleet planning, route strategy, and financial advisory opportunities.
  • Cathay Pacific — HKD 100B+ fleet investment — 100+ aircraft on order (777-9, A330-900, A321neo, A350F). 777X dependency (35 orders, first delivery 2027 if certified). A330 mid-life replacement with A330neo. Cargo fleet transitioning 747 to A350F. Scale of programme creates demand for delivery oversight and transition management.
  • Rex Airlines recovery — exited administration Dec 2025 (Air T acquisition). All-Saab 340 fleet (avg ~31 yrs); target 44 active of 57 by mid-2027. No jet ambitions. Air T's Worthington Aviation subsidiary provides Saab 340 parts/MRO globally — strategic vertical integration.

P2F Market Drivers & Outlook
  • Global air cargo record in 2025 — IATA confirmed CTK +3.4% YoY (record volume); Asia-Pacific airlines +8.4%. 2026 growth forecast: +2.6%. E-commerce "no longer dominates" — broader mix of high-value, time-critical goods now drives demand.
  • Narrowbody P2F market bifurcation — 737-800 conversions have OVER-SUPPLIED the market (26% of NB converted freighters in storage). IBA warns of declining lease rates (~US$170–180K/mo). Only ~15 737-800 conversions completed in 2025 vs booming prior years.
  • A321P2F is the growth story — larger payload (27.9t vs 23.9t) and EFW/ST Engineering orderbook of 200+ makes it the preferred next-gen NB freighter. Slots into 2027. New line at MRO Japan (Naha) from H2 2025.
  • 777-300ERSF is the strategic breakthrough — IAI FAA STC Aug 2025; first 2 delivered to Kalitta Air (via AerCap) Sep 2025. ~60 orders. ~100t payload positions it as 747F replacement. BUT: severe feedstock shortage as pax airlines retain 777-300ERs due to 777X delays.
  • 777-200ER: NO P2F programme — no STC exists for standard -200ER conversion. Low feedstock value (US$5–8M) cannot justify US$25–35M conversion investment. Lower payload potential vs -300ER. Mammoth's 777-200LRMF covers the -200LR variant only (59 built, higher value, longer range). 777-200ERs heading for retirement/parting-out, not conversion.
  • Widebody capacity crisis — 777F factory production ends 2027; 777-8F delayed to 2028+; A350F certification H2 2027 at earliest. P2F is the ONLY near-term source of new widebody freighter capacity. Avg WB freighter age: 19.6 years.
  • Key customers — AerCap Cargo (launch 777ERSF lessor), ATSG/Stonepeak (30× A330P2F; acquired Apr 2025 for US$3.1B), BBAM (40× 737-800BCF + 20+ A321P2F), Kalitta Air, Qatar Cargo, Amazon, DHL, FedEx, UPS, SF Express.

Market Intelligence — Simulator Orders
  • L3Harris / Acron Aviation transition — L3Harris completed the sale of its Commercial Aviation Solutions division to TJC L.P. on 31MAR2025 for USD 800m. Rebranded as Acron Aviation. Reality7e FFS product line continues. ~50 commercial FFS in backlog at time of sale.

Methodology & Caveats
  • Backlog figures — derived from Boeing and Airbus published monthly data. Embraer quarterly. COMAC data is less transparent and based on announced commitments which may include Memoranda of Understanding (MOUs) and Letters of Intent (LOIs) rather than firm orders.
  • Market values — indicative ranges based on industry appraisals. Actual transaction values vary significantly based on maintenance status, engine hours/cycles, interior condition, and market timing.
  • List prices — published catalogue prices. No airline pays list price; typical negotiated discounts range from 40–60% depending on order size, customer relationship, and competitive dynamics.
  • Fleet ages — based on average of active fleet only. Stored, parked, and retired aircraft are excluded. Data sources may differ slightly in their definitions of "active."
  • Production rates — targets are based on OEM guidance and are subject to supply chain conditions, regulatory approvals, and quality control requirements. Historical ramp-ups have frequently been delayed.
  • P2F data — conversion costs and timelines are approximate. Actual costs vary with aircraft condition, specification requirements, and conversion house pricing. Wait times change based on demand.
  • Limitations — this dashboard uses static data compiled from public sources. For real-time fleet tracking, live order monitoring, and verified market values, subscription databases (Cirium, IBA, ch-aviation) are recommended.
  • Simulator data — FFS inventories are compiled from CAE, Acron Aviation, FlightSafety International, Boeing, Airbus, HAVELSAN, and airline operator public sources. All devices listed as "FFS" at commercial training centres are Level D-qualified (the highest under EASA CS-FSTD(A) and FAA AC 120-40C) unless otherwise noted. CAE does not publicly name all FFS customers; of 120 FFS sold across FY2024–FY2025, the named orders in this dashboard account for approximately 20–25 units. Simulator order data represents publicly announced orders only.
